## COSIG: Collection of Open Science Integrity Guides

COSIG is a collection of expert-authored guides that aim to democratize post-publication peer review and forensic meta-science. The guides are written by domain experts and cover a wide range of topics, from image forensics and data extraction to statistical methods and plagiarism detection.

COSIG is built on four principles:

- Anyone can do post-publication peer review.
- Anyone can be a steward of the scientific literature.
- Anyone can do forensic meta-science.
- Anyone can sleuth.

### Citation

COSIG contributors. Collection of Open Science Integrity Guides. doi: [10.17605/OSF.IO/2KDEZ](https://doi.org/10.17605/OSF.IO/2KDEZ)

## Guide domains

COSIG currently contains 34 guides organized across five domains:

| Domain | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **Basics** | Post-publication peer review fundamentals, PubPeer, reporting to publishers |
| **General** | Image forensics, data extraction, citation verification, plagiarism detection, ethical approval, stealth corrections |
| **Biology & Medicine** | Antibody validation, cell line verification, nucleotide sequences, tumor measurement |
| **Materials Sciences & Engineering** | X-ray diffraction, spectroscopy, elemental composition, Tauc plots |
| **Mathematics, Statistics & Computer Science** | Binary/multiclass classifier evaluation, GRIM testing, data leakage, pseudoreplication |
